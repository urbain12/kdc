    Services

    Medical Dermatology

    Diagnoses and treats skin conditions using medical interventions like medications, targeting issues such as acne, eczema, and infections.

    Surgical Dermatology

    Specializes in surgical procedures to remove skin lesions, tumors, and abnormalities, employing techniques like excisions, Mohs surgery, and laser surgery

    Pediatric Dermatology

    Tailors dermatological care for infants, children, and adolescents, addressing specific conditions such as birthmarks, atopic dermatitis, and congenital skin disorders.

    Dermato-Allergology

    Focuses on the connection between skin disorders and allergic reactions, using diagnostic tools like patch testing to manage conditions such as contact dermatitis and urticaria.

    Specialized Laboratory

    Utilizes advanced light treatments for conditions like psoriasis and vitiligo, customizing therapy through specialized wavelengths for optimal results.

    Targeted Phototherapy

    Employs cutting-edge light treatments, tailoring therapy with specialized wavelengths to address conditions such as psoriasis and vitiligo, ensuring the best possible outcomes.

    Aesthetic Primers

    Enhances skin appearance through cosmetic procedures like chemical peels and non-invasive treatments, prioritizing aesthetic goals while maintaining skin health.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Explore our FAQs for valuable insights and answers to common questions about skin health and care at Kigali Dermatology Center, guiding you on the path to optimal well-being

    • What does a dermatologist treat?

      Dermatologists specialize in treating conditions related to the skin, hair, and nails. This includes acne, eczema, psoriasis, skin cancer, and various cosmetic concerns.

    • Can a dermatologist help with aging skin?

      Yes, dermatologists offer various treatments to address signs of aging, including wrinkles, fine lines, and sagging skin. These may include injectables, laser therapies, or topical treatments.

    • How often should I see a dermatologist?

      It depends on individual health concerns. Generally, an annual skin check is recommended to detect early signs of skin issues or cancer. However, those with specific conditions may need more frequent visits.

    • Are dermatology treatments painful?

      Most dermatological treatments are minimally invasive and relatively painless. Your dermatologist will discuss the procedure and any potential discomfort during the consultation.

    • How can I prevent skin cancer?

      Regular use of sunscreen, avoiding excessive sun exposure, and routine skin checks are essential for preventing skin cancer. Consult your dermatologist for personalized advice based on your skin type and risk factors.
